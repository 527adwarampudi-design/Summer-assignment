Marching

In marching band, you march with straight posture, holding your instrument correctly while keeping your upper body still and your head up, eyes forward. Use a roll step (heel-to-toe movement)...

Drill

In marching band, drill refers to the specific movements and formations performers follow on the field, usually mapped out as dots or coordinates on a chart. Each band member is assigned exact spots and paths to move between during a show, creating visual patterns that match the music.

Stretches

Marching band stretches are a crucial part of warm-ups that help prevent injury, improve performance, and get everyone ready to move in sync. Even though marching band isn’t usually considered a “sport” by outsiders, it requires athleticism—you’re marching, playing, and moving in all directions for long periods, often in extreme heat.
